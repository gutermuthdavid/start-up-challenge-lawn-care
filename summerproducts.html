Tips and Tricks

Summer is the toughest season for your lawn. The high temperatures and lack of rain puts extreme stress on the grass. The best thing you can do is to water the lawn and put down some fertilizer around the 4th of July holiday. If your lawn is weed infested, I recommend using the Scotts Triple Action product. If your lawn is healthy, keep it up with some iron rich fertilizer. Most lawns which do not have an in ground or above ground sprinkler system will "burn out" and go dormant by the end of August. My best advice is water the lawn if your budget allows for it and try to do your best to set it up and get your grass ready for the heat... IMPORTANT! Sharpen your mower's blade. One thing you can definitely control is how you mow. Sharpening the blade helps get a clean, straight cut which helps to reduce further stress on the grass. I hope you find all these tips and tricks useful and good luck as you take on the challenge of maintaining your lawn yourself!
