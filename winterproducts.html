Tips and Tricks

I have four bullet points under the winter season on the main page. The first one is one last mowing. IMPORTANT! Never mow the grass too short. I believe the recommended height is 4". One last mowing is recommended in November to prevent any areas from being overgrown.
I would also recommend spreading winter lawn food. It gives your lawn a better chance of survial during the cold winter months ahead. Other lawn related things to do include constatly removing excessive amounts of leaves from the lawn. Wet leaves can cause damage to the newly grown grass by pressing down and "suffocating" areas. IMPORTANT! Try NOT to rake, instead use a leaf blower/vac. Raking will tear out and weaken the newly established grass.
Lastly, DO NOT FORGET to shut down your outside taps. The winter freeze can cause serious damage to pipes...
One more thing... in the height of winter, when eveything is frozen solid, your lawn is very fragile. Simply walking on frozen grass can cause deep cell damage so try to stay off the lawn during the coldest winter months if possible.
