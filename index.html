The neighbor's lawn always seems to be greener...

I am here to help you create a healthy lawn on your own with useful tips and tricks. If you follow my guidelines, your grass will have a better look than your neighbor's. It is time to dominate the neighborhood with the greenest lawn!



(Please note the suggested guidelines on this website are ONLY intended for the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America.)

@Home Lawn Care™ all year guide:

Fall Season
(mid Sept. - end of Nov.)

Once the Summer and scorching heat come to an end, Fall is the season in the Mid-Atlantic region to "rebuild" and strengthen your lawn. There are four things you should consider and do when the overnight temperatures start to stay around 75℉:

  • Aeration
  • Overseeding
  • Starter Fertilizer
  • Watering

Products and Tips

Winter Season
(end of Nov. - mid March)

Winter is the easiest season in terms of lawn care. This is the time when real lawn enthusiasts get a mental break from the constant lawn to do list...
As overall temperatures start to drop, there are a few things to consider to get your lawn ready for the dormant period:

  • One Last Mowing
  • Winter Lawn Food Fertilizer
  • Winterize Outside Taps
  • Constantly Remove Leaves

Products and Tips

Spring Season
(mid March - mid July)

Depending on the weather in a given year, your lawn starts to come alive again between the end of February and the middle of March. This period is crucial for weed prevention and helping your grass get strong and lushly green. There are a few things to do at this time:

  • Weed Preventer Fertilizer
  • Spot Killing Weeds
  • Allowing Growth

Products and Tips

Summer Season
(mid July - mid Sept.)

Summer is the most stressful season for your lawn while temperatures reach scorching highs. Additionally, kids and pets can easily damage grass simply by playing on the already stressed lawn. Most years there is not enough rain in the summer. Eventually, your lawn goes dormant again in August. Things to consider to help your lawn in the summer:

  • Fertilizing
  • Watering
  • Mowing with Care
